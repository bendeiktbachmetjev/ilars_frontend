Effective date: 2025-11-04 · Last updated: 2025-11-04

Controller/Provider: ABBA (“we”, “us”, “our”). If you are in the EU/UK, the data controller is ABBA. The platform is developed and maintained by ABBA for clinical use. ABBA is the Data Controller and is fully responsible for data privacy and GDPR compliance. The hospital or clinic acts solely as a user of the platform.

Welcome to ABBA LARS, a mobile application designed to help individuals track bowel function and receive educational insights related to life after rectal cancer surgery, including symptoms associated with Low Anterior Resection Syndrome (LARS). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your information, in accordance with privacy regulations across the globe (EU, UK, USA, South Korea, Turkey, Russia, Australia, New Zealand, and other regions where ABBA LARS may be used).

1. What Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following types of data, exclusively with your explicit consent, and only to the extent necessary to deliver the core features of the application. No personally identifiable information (such as name, email, address, or phone number) is collected at any time. All data is stored under an automatically generated unique user code and remains pseudonymized.

Data categories include:

  • Technical identifier: Automatically generated unique user code for session continuity, device linking, and symptom tracking. You can use the same code on other devices.
  • Health and symptom-related data: Bowel movement frequency and consistency (e.g. Bristol Stool Form Scale), LARS Score, CRADI-8 Score, LARS-specific symptoms (urgency, clustering, incontinence, nocturnal leakage, incomplete evacuation), diet, fluid intake, medications, weight/height/BMI/age, lifestyle factors.
  • App interaction & usage: Time spent, features accessed, session frequency and version history.
  • We avoid tracking that profiles users. Any future crash/analytics SDKs would be configured not to collect identifiers and listed here.

2. Why We Collect Your Data

We collect and process your data only with your explicit consent and for: symptom tracking and visualizations; educational suggestions for LARS self-management; clinical insights to improve care; app improvement and quality control; compliance with privacy laws; secure cross-device functionality.

We do not collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, or IP addresses. We do not sell or share your data with third parties for advertising or marketing.

3. Legal Basis (Global Overview)

We process data on the basis of explicit, informed consent and comply with GDPR (EU/UK), HIPAA-aligned safeguards and CCPA/CPRA (USA), PIPA (South Korea), KVKK (Turkey), Federal Law 152-FZ (Russia), Privacy Act 1988 (Australia), Privacy Act 2020 (New Zealand), and other applicable laws.

4. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to: access your data; rectification; erasure (“right to be forgotten”); withdraw consent; data portability; restriction of processing; object to processing; lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. To exercise these rights, contact us (see Contact below).

5. How We Protect Your Data

We use TLS in transit, AES-256 encryption at rest, role-based access control, pseudonymization, and do not store passwords or identifiers. We use infrastructure that is HIPAA-eligible, GDPR-compliant, and ISO 27001–aligned. No system can be guaranteed fully secure; please safeguard your user code and report any suspected misuse.

6. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Data may be processed or stored in jurisdictions outside your country. We use Standard Contractual Clauses and other approved mechanisms, explicit consent where required, and data localization where mandated by law.

7. Data Retention

We retain pseudonymized data while your user code is active; upon deletion request we erase it within a reasonable time unless legally required to retain de-identified copies. Anonymized data for clinical improvement may be retained indefinitely.

ABBA LARS is not a medical device or diagnostic tool and does not provide medical advice. It does not replace professional healthcare. Users are responsible for their interpretation of the content. We advise consulting a physician before any medical or lifestyle changes. Use of the app does not create a doctor–patient relationship. Liability is limited to the extent permitted by law.
